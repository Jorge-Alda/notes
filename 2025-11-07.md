Dear Titus,
We talked a couple of days ago about the potential of using our library ALP-aca for the analysis at LHCb. I have talked with my collaborators, and we have identified two potential issues, that we are already trying to address.
First, since our code has focused so far in ALP production via meson decays, we haven't implemented yet production in p-p collisions. However, we feel that this is the natural next step in the development of ALP-aca, as it would allow us to extend our mass range up to 100 GeV. We feel confident that it can be implemented in a short time-frame, possibly in a few weeks.
The second issue is a bit more structural, and it is related to the implementation of experimental constraints. At the moment, the different constraints are essentially hard-coded, and it is necessary to modify the code to add new ones. We can imagine that this is not really practical for your use-case, and consequently, we are thinking of ways to load new measurements during runtime and/or from configuration files. In any case, once we have ready the p-p ALP production, we will add the LHCb constraint, as well as the similar searches by ATLAS.
We are very grateful for your feedback, and we hope that with your help we can make ALP-aca a useful tool for the LHCb community.
