$$\mathcal{L}_5 = \phi(C_{\phi B^2} B_{\mu\nu} \tilde B^{\mu\nu} + C_{\phi W^2} W_{\mu\nu}^I \tilde W^{\mu\nu,I}+C_{\phi G^2} G_{\mu\nu}^a \tilde G^{\mu\nu,a} + C_{\phi\ell eH} \bar\ell e H + C_{\phi quH}\bar q u \tilde H + C_{\phi qdH}\bar q d H)$$

- Individual bounds for couplings to gauge bosons:

$$|C_{\phi B^2}|\sqrt{s} \leq \sqrt{\frac{8\pi}{1+\sqrt{33}}}$$

$$|C_{\phi W^2}|\sqrt{s} \leq \sqrt{\frac{8\pi}{1+\sqrt{97}}}$$

$$|C_{\phi G^2}|\sqrt{s} \leq \sqrt{\frac{4\pi}{15}}$$

- Combined bound to all gauge bosons (see eqs (4.8)-(4.15))

- Individual bounds to chirality-flipping couplings to fermions
$$s\sum|C^{ij}_{\phi\ell e H}|^2\leq 64\pi^2 $$

$$s\sum|C^{ij}_{\phi qd H}|^2\leq \frac{64}{3}\pi^2 $$

$$s\sum|C^{ij}_{\phi qd H}|^2\leq \frac{64}{3}\pi^2 $$

- Combined bound to fermions

$$s \sum \left(|C_{\phi\ell e H}^{ij}|^2 + 3|C^{ij}_{\phi qu H}|^2 + 3|C^{ij}_{\phi qd H}|^2\right) \leq 64\pi^2$$

Is it possible to translate this bounds to the derivative basis?

$$\mathcal{L} = i \frac{a}{f_a}\frac{v+h}{\sqrt{2}} \bar u_L \tilde c_u u_R$$

$$C_{\phi qu H} = \frac{i}{\sqrt{2}f_a}\tilde c_u$$

$$\tilde c_u = \hat Y_u (R_u^\dagger c_u R_u) - (L_u^\dagger c_q L_u) \hat Y_u$$
$\hat Y_f =L_f^\dagger Y_f R_f$
